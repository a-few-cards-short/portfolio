Okay, you caught me. I've built little toys over the years, but never committed fully to making an actual, bona fide game. (Though you can check out a bit of my coding prowess in the UI Overhaul and Fingerprint Scanner examples ... and this site itself.) Instead, I want to use this opportunity to talk about AI and it's place in programming.

AI is a tool. It's the tool of the future, but it should never be the answer. Currently, you have to be very explicit with LLMs and you need to understand programming fundamentals in order to integrate AI generated code into your projects. Sometimes, generated solutions just work and we don't know exactly how. This is when students should refactor the code and break it down into more digestible chunks.

There's a saying going around that "you won't lose your job to AI, you'll lose your job to someone who uses AI." That's why I've been playing around with AI a lot lately and understand how it works and how it can augment our existing skill sets.

I used Copilot to help me make this small 2D spaceship flight toy. Please use the W A S D keys to navigate the ship.

It took a bit of coaxing, but after a while, I figured out how to describe what I wanted and broke up those requests into bite-sized pieces. Asking for all of that at once was too much for the AI though. (This was a very informative exercise for me.) The ship movement is basic, but the multi-layered, configurable, randomly dispersed, repeating parallax star field (whew!) was a bit more than I could chew so I'm glad Copilot had a strategy for that. Everything you see though had to be tweaked manually and that means understanding what is going on.

At the end of the day, if I suspected a student of using AI too heavily for an assignment, I'd be inclined to ask "How does it work? Why did you employ this particular strategy? Can you easily tweak it to work a different way? Show me, please."  I'm not concerned about how they got the answer; just if they understand it. I have pretty good spider senses for this sort of thing.

AI is a Swiss Army knife.
(Programmers need to know how to use it to survive.)
