experience

For over 20 years, I worked as a jewellery designer, goldsmith and engraver. This gave me the opportunity to work directly with paying customers on custom projects that represented meaning in their lives. Establishing needs, desires, budgets, costs and expectations was an everyday occurrence; let alone manufacturing and delivering the product.

During those years, I still maintained my programming with hobbyist endeavours mostly using JavaScript, SVG, HTML5 and CSS. My son just recently graduated from the NAIT DMIT - Videogame Programming concentration so I'm familiar with recent college curriculum and expectations.

Two decades ago, I worked in web application programming and understand team-based projects that take weeks and months to build. Balancing making progress with showing progress is critical to team morale, management, marketing and client relations. At the end of the day, the process of building software has not changed all that much. I still feel relevant.  :-)

My goal, as an instructor, is to empower students with the toolset and skills to produce quality work to completion. Clear goals, clear vision and clean execution is a mindset that must be championed. Giving students the right perspective is just as important as the right skills.
